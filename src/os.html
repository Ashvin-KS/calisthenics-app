Noun Gender & Plural Rules (Genus & Plural)

In German, every noun has a grammatical gender, indicated by its definite article. This is crucial for grammar.

  • Masculine: der (e.g., der Tisch - the table)
  • Feminine: die (e.g., die Schule - the school)
  • Neuter: das (e.g., das Buch - the book)

The plural form of nouns must be memorized. However, there are common patterns:

Common Plural Formations

Rule / Ending Explanation & Common Gender Singular Example Plural Example
-e Very common for masculine and neuter nouns. Often adds an Umlaut (ä, ö, ü). der Tisch / der Stuhl die Tische / die Stühle
-(e)n The most common plural. Used for almost all feminine nouns and many others. die Frau / die Lampe die Frauen / die Lampen
-er Common for neuter nouns. Almost always adds an Umlaut if possible. das Kind / das Buch die Kinder / die Bücher
-s Mostly for nouns from other languages (especially English) and abbreviations. das Auto / das Hobby die Autos / die Hobbys
No Change Masculine and neuter nouns ending in -er, -el, -en often don't change. der Lehrer / das Fenster die Lehrer / die Fenster

Essential Grammar for Exams (Wichtige Grammatik)

Question Formation (Fragen bilden)

W-Fragen (Information Questions): The verb is always in the second position.

  • Was studieren Sie? (What do you study?)
  • Wo liegt Berlin? (Where is Berlin located?)
  • Wie alt bist du? (How old are you?)
  • Wer ist das? (Who is that?)

Ja/Nein-Fragen (Yes/No Questions): The verb is always in the first position.

  • Haben Sie ein Auto? (Do you have a car?) -> Ja / Nein.
  • Wohnen Sie in Berlin? (Do you live in Berlin?) -> Ja / Nein.

Numbers from 21 to 99

The structure is: (unit) UND (ten). For example, 53 is "dreiundfünfzig".

  • 50: fünfzig
  • 60: sechzig
  • 70: siebzig
  • 80: achtzig
  • 81: einundachtzig
  • 99: neunundneunzig

Hundreds (Hunderter)

Structure: (number)hundert(rest of number).

  • 100: (ein)hundert
  • 200: zweihundert
  • 350: dreihundertfünfzig
  • 876: achthundertsechsundsiebzig

Thousands (Tausender) and Beyond

The same logic continues.

  • 1.000: (ein)tausend
  • 2.008: zweitausendacht
  • 5.642: fünftausendsechshundertzweiundvierzig
  • 10.000: zehntausend
  • 50.000: fünfzigtausend
  • 100.000: (ein)hunderttausend
  • 1.000.000: eine Million
